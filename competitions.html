In-person competitions

The University of Central Florida Annual High School Programming Tournament (UCF HSPT) is one of the club’s most beloved contests, taking place in the second week of March. The contest takes place over 4 hours, typically having 12-14 problems with solutions released shortly after the contest. The top five teams are awarded prizes, in addition to other goodies for participating.

Code Quest is a competition held in Lockheed Martin’s Orlando location. Code Quest is usually about 2 and a half hours and has divisions for beginner and experienced coders. The problem set is much larger than that of UCF’s, with roughly 30 problems a problem set. The problems are also ranked in order of difficulty, with harder problems awarding more points.

Online competitions

The United States of America Computing Olympiad is an online competition that consists of 3 problems that must be solved over the course of 3 and a half hours. The contest can be started at any point within a 3 day window, and performing well enough warrants a promotion into the next division. Contests are held in December, January, and February.

VJudge pulls problems from various past competitions and is used to hold club contests. We host mock competitions for our students, usually every saturday to learn algorithms and data structures.

ACSL is known as the American Computer Science League and there are various different contests throughout the year. There are multiple choice portions as well as short answer problems which are essentialy programming problems.
